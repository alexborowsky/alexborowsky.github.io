Rokas Lab at Vanderbilt University

Specialized Metabolic Gene Clusters in Plant Genomes

Under the supervision of Dr. Jen Wisecaver, I used the bioinformatics program antiSMASH to predict putative specialized biosynthetic gene clusters in a range of plant genomes. Next, I used the co-expression database ATTED-II to assess the co-expression of these putative clusters and the co-expression of known clusters. I found that very few of the predicted clusters showed patterns of co-expression while co-expression validated known clusters. In conclusion, I found that clustering is not a useful genomic feature of plant specialized metabolism in identifying novel pathways. This work is part of the publication, A Global Coexpression Network Approach for Connecting Genes to Specialized Metabolic Pathways in Plants.
